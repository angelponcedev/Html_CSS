Steps to do a good deadlift

  1. Place your feet hip apart from each other
  2. Place the barbell halfway through your foot
  3. Straighten your back as it is important not to lift with your lower back
  4. Place each one of your hands one extended palm away from the plates
  5. Make sure to have a full hand grip on the barbell, with your thumb going around it
  6. Start to lift the bar, make sure that you're using your legs and upper back as the main muscles for the lift
  7. While lifting the barbell try to lift it as close to your legs as possible without hitting yourself in the process
  8. Lift until you're back is in a straight position and the barbell is somewhere around the middle of your quads
  9. Make sure to deload with proper form and control to avoid injuring yourself

Powerlifting Essentials

Deadlift
A lift made from a standing position, without the use of a bench or other equipment.

Barbell Squat
A barbell squat is a fundamental compound exercise primarily targeting the muscles of the legs and lower back. It involves positioning a barbell across the upper back and shoulders while standing with feet shoulder-width apart or slightly wider. Keeping the chest up and the core engaged, the lifter begins the movement by bending at the knees and hips, lowering the body down until the thighs are parallel to the ground or as far as comfortably possible. The movement is then reversed, driving through the heels to lift the weight back to the starting position, fully extending the hips and knees.

Bench Press
A bodybuilding and weightlifting exercise in which a lifter lies on a bench with the feet on the floor and raises a weight with both arms.
